Research Roadmap

Hybrid molecular-spin & superconducting platforms for quantum error correction

A scientific and engineering roadmap for simulating logical qubits built from molecular spin qubits coupled through superconducting resonators — and the LindbladTTN code path that will support it.

Pitch (expert level)

We propose to combine the long coherence times and chemical tunability of vanadyl and lanthanide molecular spin qubits with the strong, controllable coupling of cQED architectures to realise cavity-mediated entangling gates in a regime tractable by tree tensor networks. The endgame is a fault-tolerant logical qubit using the surface or color code in a hybrid solid-state stack.

Status — v0.3.0 shipped

All ten code milestones M1 through M10 are implemented and validated against QuTiP. The pre-existing PS1 2× rate bug for ≥2 sites has been fixed. 27 / 27 tests pass; the Bell-state cross-resonance trajectory matches QuTiP to ~1.6 × 10⁻⁴. See the code roadmap for the milestone-by-milestone status and API.

The three pillars

Every hybrid platform discussed in this roadmap has the same three ingredients.

Molecular spin qubits

S=1/2 vanadyl complexes (VO(TPP), VOPc) and high-spin lanthanide SMMs (TbPc2, Dy-SIMs). Long T2, chemically tunable, but with small magnetic-dipole couplings to a cavity.

Pillar →

SC elements as couplers

High-Q LC/CPW resonators, with optional non-linearity from a transmon (Duffing) or fluxonium (cosφ). They mediate spin–spin interactions and provide a readout bus.

Pillar →

QEC codes & logical qubits

Surface code (rotated and unrotated) and color codes (4.8.8, 6.6.6). Stabilizer measurements via dispersive readout; lattice surgery for logical gates.

Pillar →

Why TTN? Why LindbladTTN?

Hybrid platforms have a natural star connectivity: one resonator entangles many spins. A tree tensor network places the resonator at the root and the spins at the leaves — the bond dimension only has to absorb the mediated entanglement, not direct pairwise entanglement. For dispersive regimes this keeps the bond dimension low and the simulation tractable.

Scaling

O(N·D²) vs O(16N)

Dense Liouville space needs 16^N entries. A TTN with bond dimension D only needs ~N·D². For 10 spins and D=8 that is ~2.6 kB vs ~1 TB.

Open dynamics

Native Lindblad

LindbladTTN works in interleaved Liouville space. Dissipation channels (T1, Tφ, κ) are first-class citizens — no Monte-Carlo unravelling required.

Topology

Cavity-centred tree

The roadmap adds a cavity_centered factory: bosonic nodes sit at the interior of the tree, spins at the leaves. Mirrors the physical Hamiltonian.

When TTN wins

Star and tree-like physical connectivities, dispersive regimes (mediated entanglement), and intermediate sizes (5–30 spins) are the sweet spot. Dense ME wins for N<5; MPS wins for purely 1D systems; TTN is the right tool when one or a few bosonic modes connect many qubits.
